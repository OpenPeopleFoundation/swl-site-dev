# Staff Culture at Snow White Laundry

## Summary
Staff culture at Snow White Laundry is built on the principle that care for guests cannot be authentic if care for staff is absent. The restaurant treats staff wellbeing, professional development, and psychological safety as fundamental requirements, not optional benefits.

The culture values curiosity, humility, and commitment to craft. Staff are encouraged to ask questions, to challenge assumptions, and to contribute ideas. Mistakes are treated as learning opportunities, not failures. The restaurant functions as a teaching institution, investing in staff development both within the restaurant and beyond it.

Communication is direct, respectful, and timely. Feedback is given with intention and received with openness. Conflicts are addressed promptly and constructively. The goal is to create an environment where staff can do their best work while feeling supported and valued.

## Core Principles
- Staff wellbeing is a non-negotiable priority.
- Professional development is supported and encouraged.
- Psychological safety enables honest communication.
- Mistakes are learning opportunities, not failures.
- The restaurant invests in people, not just positions.

## Signals & Behaviours
- Staff feel safe raising concerns without fear of retaliation.
- Training and development are scheduled, not improvised.
- Feedback is given and received constructively.
- Staff can explain the reasoning behind their work.
- The culture supports both individual growth and team cohesion.
